Breaking ledgerctf's ctf2 challenge

Date Thu 17 May 2018 By Axel "0vercl0k" Souchet Category reverse-engineering Tags reverse-engineering ledgerctf whitebox

Introduction

About a month ago, my mate b0n0n was working on the ledgerctf puzzles and challenged me to have a look at the ctf2 binary. Annnnd I did; this blogpost will discuss this specific challenge and the scheme you had to break. Before diving though, here is a bit of background.

ledger is a french security company started in 2014 that is specialized in cryptography, cryptocurrencies and hardware. They put up three different puzzles online to celebrate the official launch of their bug bounty program. The challenge number two called 'ctf2' is what this blogpost is going to discuss.

We are given an ELF64 binary called ctf2 available here for download for you to follow at home :). The binary is about 11MB, written in C++ and even has symbols. The below is the storyline attached to the challenge:

You're given a program which runs on a remote host. This program implements an access control based on AES.
You're asked to find the input which outputs "**** Login Successful ****".
You're not asked to modify the exec so that it outputs "**** Login Successful ****"" (it's supposed to run on my machine). It's compiled on Linux-64, and is not striped.
